So you’re thinking car accessories might just be the perfect present for that friend, boyfriend, girlfriend, partner; but what you’re not so sure about is… what makes for a good accessory in the first place? Is it something you only do to celebrate the purchase of a brand spanking new car or is giving a little love to their second hand wheels a good move as well?


Well, as a manufacturer and retailer of quality car accessories we just so happen to have a few thoughts on the topic (no surprises there, right!). So let’s unpack that a little, looking at the three essential things a good car accessory should smash out of the park every time.



Offer Protection x2

It’s a no brainer right. Your car accessory, be it a car floor mat, dashboard cover, boot liner, or seat cover etc, should protect your vehicle from the harsh effects of the sun’s UV, dirt in its various forms, and (where specified) liquid dropped by passengers. The barrier formed between these elements and your car’s original setup should be strong enough to not only withstand the bad stuff, but do it for a reasonable amount of time. Something your trusty friends at the local consumer watchdog will keep an eye on for you.


But where does the ‘x2’ come into play I hear you ask? Well an awesome car accessory should help to keep you safe (or better put, not impede your car’s inbuilt safety features) while you get from A to B. You might have heard cases in the media where some poor soul has a car accident because of some incorrectly sized car accessory or due to a fitment mistake. So the kind of issues to watch out for include:


  • Car mat sliding forward and causing the car to accelerate.
    Cause: no retention system in place

  • Car mats not fitting correctly and causing the car to accelerate.
    Cause: not made to fit

  • Dash mats that do not easily allow air bag deployment.
    Cause: safety design fault

  • Seat covers that do not easily allow air bag deployment.
    Cause: safety design fault

  • ...among others



Enhance

If there’s one thing Balenciaga’s leather skirt tells us, it’s that we all have different ideas about what looks killer. And when it comes to car accessories, that fact doesn’t change. Some like their accessories to look like black or grey factory (OEM) gear that neutrally blends in, while others like to add a little spice to their colour pallette. But if there is one thing we all share, it’s that we all want our cars to look well kept.


The desire to improve the look of your car is especially strong for the second hand car owner. Their wheels carry the scar from that time your Big Mac sauce hit the deck and the immovable stain the kid left behind (I’ll leave this one to your imagination… but let’s just presume we’re talking about milk here). So car accessories are a great way to enhance the look of the car and affordably restore back to its former glory!


That’s not to say new car owners don’t love accessories. They do. In fact to “celebrate” their new purchase, car buyers around the globe cave in to ridiculously high accessory prices from their local dealer. All done to maintain or enhance the look of their car. So yes, a gift for a new car buyer is defo a good thing to do!


So when you shop, check out the retailers that give you some personalisations options and don’t cost you an arm and a leg. I think we might know one *cough*.



Drive

At the end of the day, car accessories are designed to be used. So a good accessory should make life easier and more enjoyable. They should stop glare from getting in your eyes or put your phone within eye-scan. They should make you stress less about protecting your car and more about enjoying the experiences your car brings. So get there, and grab that gift. They (and you) won’t regret it.
